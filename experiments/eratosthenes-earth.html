Measuring Earth's Circumference Experiment

Recreate Eratosthenes' Method

Learn how Eratosthenes calculated the Earth's circumference over 2000 years ago.

Steps:

  1. Measure the shadow of a vertical stick at noon on the summer solstice.
  2. Compare this measurement with the shadow (or lack thereof) in a city directly north or south.
  3. Calculate the angle difference between the two locations.
  4. Use the distance between the two cities to estimate Earth's circumference.

Discover how accurate ancient measurements could be with simple observations!
